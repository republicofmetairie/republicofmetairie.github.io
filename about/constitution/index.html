The Constitution of the Sovereign Republic of Metairie

Preamble

        This Constitution is ordained by the signees and citizens of the Sovereign Republic of Metairie in effort to create a free and safe land for all peoples. The Sovereign Republic of Metairie is hereby to be referred to as Metairie or as the Republic.

Article I - The Executive

§1        All executive powers shall be granted to the Premier of Metairie, who shall be a citizen of this Nation.

§2        The Premier of Metairie shall hold a term of Four Years and shall be elected as follows;
Every citizen shall have the right to elect their Premier of Metairie on the Election Day of June the First in the fourth year of the term of Premier. Each citizen may choose to vote by ballot for a candidate for the Office of Premier. This Candidate must be a Citizen of the Republic. The Candidate that has received the largest number of Votes shall be inaugurated Premier on the day of October First of the same year. If there should be two or more that possess the same number of votes, the winner shall be chosen by meeting of the  Lower Congress of the Assembly of the Republic.
The term of Premier shall be shared by the Prime Minister of Metairie, who shall be appointed by the Higher Congress of the Assembly of the Republic, this Appointing must be done between the dates of July First and October First of the year of election.
No Premier nor Prime Minister shall serve more than 2 terms total in their respective office.

§3        The Premier of Metairie shall have the power to sign congressionally approved bills into law, to veto congressionally approved bills, to attend diplomatic meetings on behalf of the Republic, to ensure laws be executed faithfully, to pardon any but themself of a federal crime, to appoint the Justices of the Supreme Court, to establish and administer a Cabinet, to appoint Secretaries of the Cabinet, to recognize foreign governments, to negotiate treaties on behalf of the Republic, and to suggest a law to the Lower Congress.

§4        The Prime Minister of Metairie shall have the power to lead in meetings of the Higher Congress, to act as a vote only in the event of a tie between the Congressmen, to advise the Premier in all matters, and to attend diplomatic meetings on behalf of the Republic.

§5        In the event of a national emergency or other crisis, the powers of the Premier and Prime Minister are expanded. The Prime Minister gains the ability to put Emergency Executive Bills into place, these are temporary laws that help with the crisis. In the case of a military emergency or crisis, the Premier gains the authority of the Army and may act as Commander-in-Chief of the Army for the length of the crisis. The Premier gains their emergency power in the case of any armed conflict or war, not only emergencies.

§6        A national emergency is any circumstance in which the lives and/or livelihoods of the citizens are at risk, including military threat and natural disaster. This also includes major economic disasters.

Article II - The Legislature

§1        All legislative power shall herein be granted to the Assembly of Metairie, which shall be formed of a Higher Congress and Lower Congress.

§2        The Assembly shall have the power to create and approve bills to be signed into law, to declare war upon other nations on behalf of the Republic, to sign extranational treaties on behalf of the Republic, to regulate commerce both internal and external, to establish standard weights and measures of the Republic, to establish and mint the Coin of the Republic, to establish and support an Army; Navy; Air Force; and Coast Guard, to approve bills of budget of the Republic, to create and approve all laws necessary for the continued sustaining of the Republic, to make all Laws which shall be necessary and proper for carrying into Execution the foregoing Powers; and all other Powers vested by this Constitution in the Government of the Republic; or in any Department or Officer thereof, to Declare and Remove National Emergency Status of the Republic, to exercise powers over the international borders of the Republic, and to establish Postal Offices and Roads.

§3        The Higher Congress shall be made up of two members from each Province with a term of six years and who is chosen by the Legislature of the respective Province. If any vacancy may happen by resignation or otherwise the legislature of the Province that chose this Congressman must take a meeting to choose a next one who shall have a term the length of six years. No member may be given more than 2 terms in the Higher Congress.

§4        The Lower Congress shall be made up of exactly Ninety-Five members. Each Province shall be given a number of Lower Congressmen by the percentage of the Republic’s Citizens living there according to the Census held every 5 years. The Lower Congressmen shall have a term of 4 years and shall be chosen two years after the Premier’s election on the day of November Tenth. Every candidate must assign themselves a Party to run with and every Citizen may vote, on November Tenth, for the party of their choice. Based on the percentage of votes each party gets, they will get that percent of the congressional seats.  Each party gets their number of seats and chooses their congressman to sit in it. No member shall be given more than three terms in the Lower Congress

§5        All bills must originate in the Lower Congress and may only pass to the Higher Congress after a majority of the Lower Congressmen have voted for it to be moved. Once a bill enters the Higher Congress it may be edited until the Higher Congress votes for it to be approved. After this approval, the Lower Congress holds a final vote in the newly modified bill, and will send it back to the Higher Congress if they find it unsuitable but fixable, send it to the Premier if they find it suitable, and erase the bill if they find it unfixable. Once a bill arrives on the desk of the Premier, it must be signed for approval or veto in the next 3 weeks or it will be sent back to the Higher Congress for them to vote on its ratification. If the Premier signs a bill into law, it is ratified according to the bill. After ratification of any bill, that bill may be subject to a Tribunal of the Supreme Court which may forcefully remove any unconstitutional bill from law if and only if the Supreme Court wishes to hold a Tribunal.

Article III - The Provinces

§1        No province may declare war, sign extranational treaties, create laws that contradict the law of this constitution; the executive; or the Assembly, establish any tax that be considered discriminatory.

§2        All Provinces shall possess a Provincial Assembly to establish Provincial Laws, a Provincial Constitution to establish the Province, an elected President of the Province to act as an executive, and a Provincial Police force to act upon both Provincial and National laws.

§3        Provincial Assemblies shall follow the model of the Assembly of the Republic but the Province may change the number of members in each Congress.

§4        Members of the Provincial Assemblies are chosen by a method ratified in the Provincial Constitution.

§5 The Republic shall guarantee to every Province in this Union a Republican Form of Government, and shall protect each of them against Invasion; and on Application of the Provincial Legislature, or of the Provincial Executive, against domestic Violence. The National Law shall always supersede Provincial Law in matters of National Defense, International Commerce, and the Maintenance of the Coin. No Province may create a Law that Denies the application of the Law of the Republic.

Article IV - The Supreme Court

§1        The Judicial Power of the Republic shall be vested in the Supreme Court of Metairie

§2        The Supreme Court is made up of 15 justices where each is appointed from a Lower Court by the Premier. These Justices have a term lasting as long as they live and the Premier may only select a new Justice in the case of vacancy.

§3        The Supreme Court has final authority over the following of this Constitution and may hold Tribunals to interpret the laws and rules of this constitution in context of an action performed by the Congress, Premier, or a Province.

§4        Lower Courts are Provincial Supreme Courts and Public Courts, these courts are established by the Provinces and must follow all decisions carried out by this Supreme Court. Until a decision is made by the Supreme Court, the mandate of the highest court is followed nationally.

Article V - Citizens and Rights

§1        All peoples born in the Republic are citizens by birthright, a right that cannot be stripped from a human being. All people born to one or more parents that are citizens may also become a citizen at request of the parent or of the child after the child turns 16. All peoples that are not citizens may reside in the Republic for no more than 8 years without applying for citizenship from the Government. Naturalization is the process through which the person applies for citizenship and this application is considered and acted upon by the Assembly and an Assembly-created agency.

§2        All people, both citizens and foreigners, have certain inalienable rights that may not be taken from them. These are the right to freely speak on all matters, the right to freely publish, the right to peaceful protest, the right to bear arms for defense of self or property, the right to own property, the right to naturalize, the right to work, the right to live, the right to happiness, the right to have say in government, and the right to bear witness. These rights do not include any violent crime or other crime or law that may prohibit actions that bring harm to the public. Only citizens may vote in elections both national and provincial.

§3        No law, national; provincial; or otherwise, may restrict the rights of the people, both citizens and foreigners, under any circumstance.

§4        In the event that a citizen wishes to gain citizenship in another nation, they are permitted to do so while maintaining Metairie citizenship as long as they reside in the Republic for at least one month out of every 2 years. In the event that a citizen wishes to renounce citizenship, they must request it be removed through the Assembly or an Assembly-created agency.

Article VI - Change

§1        This Constitution is not perfect nor is it complete, therefore the Assembly may vote at any time to choose to amend it. Any amendment made is synonymous with the document of the Constitution and must be approved by both Congresses of the Assembly at a 65% majority, the Premier of Metairie, and at least 50% of provincial Assemblies at 50% majority. These amendments are not to be direct alterations of this Constitution but extensions of it that may override other parts of this Constitution.

Article VII - Higher Powers

§1        This nation resides on the land of The United States of America and its state of Louisiana. The Republic acknowledges the existence and recognized authority of the United States and Louisiana over the land and people. This Republic does not claim ownership above the United States but intends to act beneath them until the day that the Republic may be truly free and sovereign.

§2        This Article and its Sections are to be unratified at the moment the Sovereign Republic of Metairie gains independence if that ever is to happen.

Article VIII - Interim

§1        For the duration that this Republic does not have the amount of people needed to form a proper government there shall be no Assembly but instead a temporary 6-member Parliament consisting of the 6 most voted-for candidates in the election, which shall be held on the same day as the Premier election. The Parliament will appoint a Prime Minister and unilaterally send laws to the Premier’s desk. The Prime Minister will act as a tie-breaker once again in this Parliament if a tie is to occur and leads the meetings. Upon the Parliament voting this nation large and stable enough to transition, the Republic will ratify the rest of this Constitution into effect. Articles I, III, IV, V, VI, VII, and VIII are in virtually full effect excepting the aforementioned changes in this section.
